Brief bio: I'm a computer scientist by training, with a Ph.D. and M.S. from the University of Massachusetts Amherst, and a B.A. from Harvard. My research has been in the areas of data science (a.k.a. data mining), machine learning, network science, and computational social science.

I've worked on a diverse range of projects. They've touched on Twitter, fake news, inferring social ties, anomaly and fraud detection, near-duplicate detection and data linking, information retrieval and text processing, relational learning, and bioinformatics. Here are some aspects of research I tend to get excited about: insights about math, statistics or data that can be translated into algorithms; data that's represented as a graph; understanding behavior through simple models (and baselines); and manipulating real data to learn something new about the world.
